Direction A — Eclipse

Symbol. A perfect circular disc with a smaller concentric circle removed at the center — an "eclipse" or coin. The outer ring is solid amber (Accent 500); the inner negative space takes the surface color it sits on (cream on cream, navy on navy). Geometry: outer diameter equals the cap-height of the wordmark; ring thickness is exactly 22% of the outer diameter. The mark is a closed, complete form — no open shapes, no broken curves — to signal coverage, completeness, and a policy fully in effect.

Wordmark. Set in Instrument Serif Regular, true italic disabled. Cap-line tracking −2%; the "R" terminals are kept long for character. The descriptor "SEGUROS" sits to the right baseline-aligned with the wordmark cap-height, in Geist Mono Medium at 28% of wordmark size, letter-spacing +32%, set in Ink/3.

Rationale. Argentine insurance is dominated by shields, road silhouettes, and aggressive sans-serifs. Eclipse refuses those tropes. The serif wordmark carries the formality of a legal document; the geometric mark carries the precision of a guarantee. Together they read as "a credentialed advisor who is also a modern product." The amber ring is warm enough to feel human and bright enough to function as a UI accent without ever being mistaken for an alert.

Forbidden uses

  • Do not rotate or skew
    The eclipse must remain perfectly upright. Tilt implies imbalance — fatal for an insurance brand.
  • Do not place on photographic backgrounds without scrim
    If the logo must appear over imagery, place it on a Marine 700 plate at 92% opacity with 24 px padding.
  • Do not recolor the wordmark with the accent
    The amber is reserved for the ring and for UI energy. An all-amber wordmark reads as warning.
  • Do not collapse the SEGUROS descriptor onto the wordmark baseline
    It is positioned at cap-height intentionally to read as a credential, not a continuation.

Direction B — Letterform Seal

Symbol. An 88×88 px rounded-square plate, 14 px corner radius, filled Primary 700. A thin amber inner-rule sits 6 px from the edge at 40% opacity. The letterform R is set in Instrument Serif at 56 px, weight 400, color Accent 500. The plate functions as a hallmark stamp; the inner-rule references certification documents.

Wordmark. "Ramsar" in Instrument Serif Regular, 32 px, color Primary 700. Below: "SEGUROS · ARG." in Geist Mono Medium 10.5 px, tracking +28%, Ink/3. Vertical leading between the two: 4 px optical.

Rationale. Where Eclipse is geometric, Seal is heraldic. It is the version of Ramsar that would look at home on a notarized contract or embossed on a leather portfolio. Use this direction only if user research surfaces a strong preference for traditional/institutional cues over modern minimalism.

Direction C — Stamp & Track

Symbol. A 120 px circular stamp: outer ring is a 1 px dashed stroke in Primary 700 carrying rotating descriptor text ("CERTIFICADO · DESDE 2026 ·" set in Geist Mono Medium 10 px, +18% tracking) along the path. Inside, an 80 px solid Primary 700 disc with the monogram "RS" set in Instrument Serif Italic at 40 px, Accent 500. Diameter ratio outer:inner is 3:2, fixed.

Rationale. The most expressive of the three. § 02 / VII

Color System

A two-temperature palette: a deep marine teal as the brand anchor, balanced by a warm amber for energy. Every value is locked. Every contrast ratio is tested against WCAG 2.2 AA.

Brand · Primary Marine

Marine — the brand's center of gravity

Hue rationale. A blue-leaning teal sits between two ideas the category lives in: the formality of navy (banks, law firms, traditional insurers) and the modernity of teal (digital products, fintech). It is the color of a calm harbor — not the blue of authority, but the blue-green of safe arrival. In the Argentine market — saturated with primary blues (Banco Provincia, YPF, AFA) and reds (Banco Galicia, Movistar) — marine carves out an unowned, premium space. Primary 700 is the deployable workhorse: it passes 7.2:1 against white, comfortably exceeding AAA for body text.

Brand · Secondary Midnight

Midnight — the quiet authority

Relationship to primary. Midnight is a desaturated, near-black navy. It is intentionally less chromatic than Marine so the two never compete: Marine carries the brand voice, Midnight handles weight (footers, dark-mode chrome, dense data surfaces). The pair reads as "marine on a moonless night" — same world, different role. Midnight 700 is the safe pairing color for amber accents that need a high-contrast bed.

Brand · Accent Amber

Amber — energy without alarm

Why amber, not red or orange. Red is reserved for errors and danger — using it as an accent would erode validation feedback. Pure orange reads cheap and discount-y (a brand-trust hazard in insurance). Amber sits between gold and orange: it borrows gold's premium, hand-finished quality and orange's warmth, without the loudness of either. Against Marine 700 it generates a contrast of 5.94:1 — high enough to function as a CTA on dark surfaces, low enough to feel inviting rather than emergency. The accent appears at roughly 5–8% of any composition; if you see more, redesign.

Typography System

Two voices: a serif that speaks, a sans that explains. The serif handles emotional moments — promises, headlines, brand affirmations. The sans handles everything that has to be read at length, scanned, or filled in.

Why. Instrument Serif is contemporary, generously open, and has a beautifully restrained italic. Its high stroke contrast carries the formality insurance demands; its rounded terminals soften that formality just enough to feel modern. We use a single weight (400) and let scale do the rest of the work — a deliberate constraint that keeps every headline coherent.

§ 06 / VII

Iconography

Lucide, set in outline at 1.75 px stroke, recolored to Marine 700 by default. Decision is final: a single library, a single weight, a single style across the product.

Library recommendation

Choice: Lucide. Lucide is permissively licensed (ISC), maintained as a community fork of Feather with active growth (~1,500 icons), and crucially — its geometry uses a consistent 1.5 px stroke on a 24 px artboard. We override stroke to 1.75 px to better fit Geist's weight and match Instrument Serif's stroke contrast.

Outline, not filled. Filled icon sets read corporate-utility (Material) or fintech-flat (Phosphor Fill). Outlined icons sit beside serif headlines without competing — they read as marginalia, not graphics. The only exception: active-state navigation items use a filled variant to indicate selection.

Forbidden alternates. No mixing with Heroicons, Tabler, Material, or custom SVGs. If Lucide doesn't have an icon, request it upstream or draw a custom one to Lucide's geometric rules (24 px artboard, 1.75 px stroke, 2 px round caps, 2 px corner radius, geometric construction).

Motion & Animation

Motion in Ramsar is restrained, purposeful, and respects the user's attention. Every animation answers one question: did something change, and where? If it doesn't, it doesn't ship.

Easing curves

ease-in
cubic-bezier0.55, 0.06, 0.68, 0.19
Entering elements that will then exit (toasts coming in before they live, drawer about to close).
ease-out ★
cubic-bezier0.22, 1, 0.36, 1
The system default. UI appearing, cards lifting on hover, modal opens, toast enters. Use unless reason not to.
ease-in-out
cubic-bezier0.65, 0, 0.35, 1
State changes (tab switching, accordion expand/collapse, theme toggle, page transitions).
ease-spring
cubic-bezier0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1
Attention moments only: successful quote, badge appearance, milestone counter. Rationed — once per session.

Duration scale

duration-instant0 msNo transition. Used for hit-area state where motion would feel laggy (button-press :active scale).

Animation patterns — by element

  • Button hover
    Background-color shift from Marine 700 → Marine 600 over 120 ms ease-out. Translate: none. Box-shadow elevation-0 → elevation-1 over the same duration. On :active, instant scale(0.985) (no transition) for tactile press, then restore in 120 ms.
  • Card hover (lift)
    translateY(-2 px) and box-shadow elevation-0 → elevation-2, both at 220 ms ease-out. No scale, no rotation. The lift is the entire vocabulary.
  • Dropdown / menu open & close
    Open: opacity 0 → 1 and translateY(-4 px) → 0 over 220 ms ease-out; transform-origin top. Close: opacity 1 → 0 and translateY 0 → -4 px over 120 ms ease-in. Closing is always faster than opening — exits should feel decisive.
  • Accordion expand & collapse
    grid-template-rows 0fr → 1fr over 360 ms ease-in-out for the body container. The chevron rotates 0 → 180 deg synchronously. Body contents themselves do not fade — the height reveal is the entire animation.
  • Page transition (client-side route change)
    Outgoing page: opacity 1 → 0 + translateY 0 → -8 px, 220 ms ease-in. Incoming page: opacity 0 → 1 + translateY 8 px → 0, 360 ms ease-out, 80 ms delay. Total: 460 ms. Scroll-restoration: instant to top, no animated scroll.
  • Skeleton loading → content reveal
    Skeletons use a 1.4 s shimmer: linear-gradient sweep at cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.6, 1) infinite. On content arrival, skeleton fades out (opacity 1 → 0, 220 ms ease-out); content fades in (opacity 0 → 1, 360 ms ease-out, 80 ms delay). No layout shift — skeletons share exact dimensions with eventual content.
  • Toast / alert appearance & dismissal
    Enter: from translateY(16 px) + opacity 0 to translateY(0) + opacity 1, 220 ms ease-out. Hold: 5 s (success), 8 s (error), persistent (warning with action). Dismiss: opacity 1 → 0 + translateX(16 px), 160 ms ease-in.
  • Modal open & close
    Backdrop: opacity 0 → 0.64, 220 ms ease-out. Surface: opacity 0 → 1 + scale(0.96) → scale(1) + translateY(8 px) → 0, 360 ms ease-out, 60 ms delay. Close: reverse, but 220 ms ease-in, no delay. Body scroll locked during open.
  • Number counter (stats)
    Triggered when element enters viewport (IntersectionObserver, threshold 0.5). Duration 600 ms ease-out. Easing applied to the numeric value, not via interpolation between rendered digits. Mono font with tabular numerals prevents horizontal shift during count-up. Final value held — no overshoot, no bounce.
  • Form field focus
    Border-color sand-300 → Marine 700 and 0 → 3 px Marine 700 @ 18% box-shadow halo, both at 120 ms ease-out. No label motion, no input scaling. The ring does the entire job.
  • Quote-success "celebration"
    The one place ease-spring is sanctioned. Check-circle icon: scale 0.6 → 1.0 over 600 ms ease-spring, with a 0 → 1 amber radial-gradient pulse behind it (220 ms ease-out). Used at end of cotizador only. No confetti — that would betray the brand's seriousness.

Reduced motion strategy

When @media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) is true, the following global rules apply, in order:

  1. Strip all transform-based motion
    translateY, scale, rotate animations are set to transform: none and animation: none. The visual outcome (final state) appears instantly.
  2. Preserve opacity transitions, halved
    Opacity changes are kept — they communicate state without inducing motion sickness — but durations are halved (e.g. 220 ms → 110 ms). This is the only motion-related transition retained.
  3. Remove all looping/decorative motion
    Skeleton shimmer, hero parallax, infinite-marquee testimonial tickers, ambient background gradients — all paused. Skeletons remain visible as static blocks.
  4. Counter animations: jump to final
    Stat counters render their final value immediately on viewport entry. No count-up.
  5. Accordions, modals, dropdowns: instant
    Height/scale transitions removed; open/close states snap. The information is more important than the choreography of revealing it.
  6. Hover-state color shifts: retained
    Color changes do not induce motion-related issues; they are kept at 0 ms (instant) so that interactivity remains legible.
  7. Focus rings: always visible, never animated
    Even in default motion settings, focus rings appear instantly. This rule restates the system default for clarity.
